# Acknowledgments and AI Assistance Disclosure

## Tools and Assistance Used

This project was developed with the assistance of AI-based tools. In the spirit of academic transparency, the following disclosure describes the nature and extent of AI assistance used.

### AI-Assisted Components

**Code Development:**  
AI tools (large language models) were used to assist with writing and debugging Python code for the statistical analysis, including Monte Carlo simulation, Bayesian MCMC sampling, copula fitting, and Sobol sensitivity analysis. The author provided the research direction, problem formulation, and data sources; AI tools assisted with translating methodological concepts into working code.

**Documentation:**  
AI tools were used to assist with writing the notebook documentation, including section descriptions, methodology explanations, and result interpretations. The structured Objective/Result Insights documentation format was generated with AI assistance.

**Literature Review:**  
The author identified the core references (particularly Walid *et al.* (2024) and Sankoh *et al.* (2022)) based on personal research interest. AI tools assisted with identifying additional supporting references and contextualising the methodology within the broader literature.

### Author's Independent Contributions

- **Problem identification and motivation:** The research question emerged from the author's personal experience growing up in Sierra Leone and observing energy poverty firsthand.
- **Study design:** Selection of Sierra Leone as a case study, choice of study locations, and definition of the mini-grid system parameters.
- **Data source selection:** Identification of NASA POWER, PVGIS-SARAH2, and ERA5 as appropriate data sources.
- **Critical evaluation:** Review of AI-generated code and results for physical plausibility and consistency.

### Author's Ongoing Learning

The author is currently developing deeper understanding of several advanced methods used in this analysis, including:
- Bayesian inference and MCMC sampling theory
- Copula-based dependence modelling
- Variance-based global sensitivity analysis (Sobol method)
- Stochastic optimisation under uncertainty

This project represents a learning journey, not a claim of mastery over these techniques. The author intends to develop genuine expertise in these methods through formal PhD study.
